Hi, I am Jin Hyung Lee, a Postdoctoral Researcher at [Purdue University](https://www.purdue.edu/), working with Professor [Anindya Bhadra](https://www.stat.purdue.edu/~bhadra/).

I received my Ph.D. in Statistics from the Department of Statistics at [George Mason University](https://www.gmu.edu/) in October 2025, under the supervision of [Dr. Ben Seiyon Lee](https://sites.google.com/view/benslee/). During Summer 2023 and Summer 2024, I worked as a biostatistician intern at [Astellas Pharma US](https://www.astellas.com/us/), where I applied Bayesian methods to improve efficiency and rigor in clinical trial design and analysis. Prior to my Ph.D., I have a unique industry background where I was a Senior Researcher at the [Korea International Trade Association](https://www.kita.org/). I received an M.S. in Biostatistics from the School of Public Health at [Seoul National University](https://health.snu.ac.kr/en/) under the supervision of [Dr. Ho Kim](https://health.snu.ac.kr/en/snu__professor/%EA%B9%80%ED%98%B8/), and a B.A. in Applied Statistics and Economics from [Yonsei University](https://www.yonsei.ac.kr/en_sc/index.jsp).

My research focuses on developing scalable variational Bayes methods for non-conjugate spatial models, bridging modern machine learning techniques with Bayesian methodology. I design novel variational inference frameworks that enable fast and accurate posterior inference in settings where traditional MCMC methods are computationally infeasible, particularly for large-scale spatial generalized linear mixed models. My recent work integrates neural networks and normalizing flows with variational inference to achieve substantial computational gains while maintaining strong predictive performance, with applications in atmospheric science, hydrology, and genetics.
